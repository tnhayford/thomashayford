Business isn't just taught in classrooms. It's shared in conversations, marketplaces, and moments of impact.✨️

As part of the KSB@20 Anniversary Celebration, we took our business outreach to the local marketplace last Friday. It was more than a visit. It was a meaningful exchange.

We engaged the market women on basic financial literacy, record keeping, and the importance of saving and reinvesting in their businesses. Their openness to learn and share their experiences was truly inspiring. 💡

One moment that stood out was a heartfelt conversation with a fruit seller who shared her challenge of dealing with perishable goods, especially fruits and potatoes going bad too quickly. I proposed turning the fruits into fresh juice or identifying local juice makers she could supply to. For her potatoes, we discussed linking up with small-scale potato chip producers to reduce waste and boost her income.

I also met another market woman who had no record of her customers no follow-ups, no tracking, just faith that they would return. We spoke about the importance of customer retention and follow-ups in growing a small business. To help her take the first step, I promised to provide her with an exercise book and a pen so she could begin tracking her customer interactions and sales.

These conversations reminded me that sometimes, real business transformation, starts with simple tools, a listening ear, and a little encouragement.

Proud to be part of the KSB legacy that extends beyond campus walls.💫
